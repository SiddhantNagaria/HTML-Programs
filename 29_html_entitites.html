HTML Character Entities Some characters are reserved in HTML. If you use the less than (<) or greater than (>) signs in your HTML text, the browser might mix them with tags. Entity names or entity numbers can be used to display reserved HTML characters. To display a less than sign (<) we must write: < or < To display a greater than sign (>) we must write: > or > Non-breaking Space A commonly used HTML entity is the non-breaking space:   A non-breaking space is a space that will not break into a new line. Two words separated by a non-breaking space will stick together (not break into a new line).
